Types of Hackers & How They Work

1. White Hat Hackers (Ethical Hackers)

White Hat hackers are cybersecurity professionals who use their skills to help organizations stay safe from attacks. They perform penetration testing, vulnerability scanning, and risk assessments. Their work is legal and authorized. They often hold certifications like CEH (Certified Ethical Hacker) or OSCP.

2. Black Hat Hackers (Criminal Hackers)

Black Hat hackers exploit systems for personal, financial, or political gain using malware, phishing, and ransomware. Their activities are illegal and tracked by cybersecurity agencies.

3. Grey Hat Hackers

Grey Hat hackers explore system vulnerabilities without permission but don’t intend to harm. They may inform the company or ask for a reward after reporting a flaw.

4. Script Kiddies

Script Kiddies use pre-made tools without deep knowledge. They attack systems for fun or attention, often causing damage unintentionally.

5. Hacktivists

Hacktivists are politically or socially motivated hackers. They may deface websites, leak data, or protest online for a cause.

6. Blue Hat Hackers

Blue Hat hackers are invited by organizations to test systems for bugs before launch. They may also refer to revenge-seeking hackers.

7. Red Hat Hackers

Red Hat hackers target black hats aggressively, aiming to destroy their systems or expose them. Their actions are vigilante-style and usually unauthorized.
